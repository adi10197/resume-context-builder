# Tech Lead Resume Type

This resume type is optimized for technical leadership positions balancing hands-on technical work with team leadership and cross-functional collaboration.

## Specific Rules

### Target Audience
- Tech Lead positions
- Engineering Manager roles with hands-on coding
- Staff/Principal Engineer roles with team influence
- Architect roles with team oversight

### Emphasis Areas
1. **Leadership Impact** - Team size, mentoring, technical direction
2. **Technical Strategy** - Architecture decisions, technical roadmap
3. **Cross-functional Collaboration** - Working with product, design, stakeholders
4. **Technical Excellence** - Still maintain technical credibility
5. **Business Outcomes** - Connect technical work to business value

### Section Order
1. Header (Name and Contact Info)
2. Professional Summary (recommended - highlights leadership + technical blend)
3. Professional Experience
4. Technical Skills (after experience, less prominent than IC resume)
5. Education
6. Certifications (optional)

## Professional Summary Format

Include a 2-3 sentence summary highlighting:
- Years of experience
- Leadership scope
- Technical specialties
- Key achievements

```markdown
## Professional Summary

Technical leader with 8+ years of experience leading engineering teams and architecting scalable systems. Led teams of 5-10 engineers while maintaining hands-on contributions to backend services serving 5M+ users. Expert in distributed systems, cloud architecture, and building high-performing engineering cultures.
```

## Experience Bullet Point Guidelines

### Prioritize
- Team leadership and mentoring
- Technical decision-making and architecture
- Cross-functional collaboration
- Business impact and metrics
- Process improvements
- Technical strategy and roadmap
- Scaling teams and systems
- Still include technical achievements (maintain credibility)

### Balance
Mix of leadership bullets (60%) and technical bullets (40%):
- Start with leadership/impact bullets
- Follow with key technical contributions
- Emphasize outcomes over activities

### Example Bullet Points (Leadership)
- Led team of 7 engineers delivering 5 major features per quarter, maintaining 98% on-time delivery rate
- Mentored 3 junior engineers to mid-level promotions within 12 months through 1-on-1s and code reviews
- Collaborated with Product and Design to define technical roadmap, reducing feature delivery cycle by 30%
- Established engineering best practices including code review standards and testing guidelines
- Drove architectural decision to adopt microservices, improving deployment frequency from weekly to daily

### Example Bullet Points (Technical)
- Designed and implemented event-driven architecture processing 100K+ events/second using Kafka
- Optimized database architecture reducing query latency by 50% and infrastructure costs by $200K/year

## Technical Skills Section Format

Still include but make it more focused on leadership-relevant skills:
```markdown
## Technical Skills

**Leadership**: Team building, mentoring, technical strategy, cross-functional collaboration, agile processes
**Architecture**: Microservices, distributed systems, cloud architecture, system design
**Languages**: Python, JavaScript, Go (list fewer, focus on current)
**Infrastructure**: AWS, Kubernetes, Docker, CI/CD
```

## Projects/Initiatives Section (Optional)

Highlight major initiatives you led:
```markdown
## Key Initiatives

**Microservices Migration** (2023-2024)
Led team of 8 engineers in migrating monolithic application to microservices architecture
- Improved deployment frequency from monthly to daily
- Reduced time-to-market for new features by 40%
- Decreased production incidents by 35%
```

## Job Description Tailoring

When job description is provided:
- Match leadership competencies mentioned
- Emphasize similar team size/scope
- Highlight relevant technical domains
- Show progression to leadership
- Feature collaboration with mentioned stakeholders (product, design, etc.)
- Use metrics that align with job requirements

## What to Balance
- Don't go too heavy on management (this isn't pure EM role)
- Maintain technical credibility with concrete technical achievements
- Show business acumen - connect technical work to business outcomes
- Demonstrate both "doing" and "multiplying" through others

## Career Progression Story

Show clear progression:
- Earlier roles: More technical focus
- Recent roles: Leadership + technical balance
- Demonstrate growth in scope and impact
