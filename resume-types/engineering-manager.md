# Engineering Manager Resume Type

This resume type is optimized for Engineering Management positions with focus on people leadership, team building, and organizational impact.

## Specific Rules

### Target Audience
- Engineering Manager positions
- Director of Engineering roles
- VP of Engineering roles
- Head of Engineering positions
- Development Manager roles

### Emphasis Areas
1. **People Leadership** - Hiring, mentoring, performance management, retention
2. **Team Building** - Scaling teams, culture, organizational design
3. **Delivery & Execution** - Shipping products, hitting milestones, process improvement
4. **Strategic Thinking** - Roadmap planning, technical strategy, cross-functional alignment
5. **Business Acumen** - Connecting engineering work to business outcomes
6. **Technical Credibility** - Enough technical depth to lead engineers effectively

### Section Order
1. Header (Name and Contact Info)
2. Professional Summary (recommended - highlights leadership philosophy)
3. Professional Experience
4. Leadership Skills (instead of purely technical skills)
5. Education
6. Certifications (optional - leadership training, etc.)

## Professional Summary Format

Include a 2-3 sentence summary highlighting leadership approach:
```markdown
## Professional Summary

Engineering leader with 10+ years of experience building and scaling high-performing engineering teams. Grew teams from 5 to 40+ engineers while maintaining high engagement scores and low attrition. Passionate about developing engineers' careers, fostering inclusive cultures, and delivering products that delight users.
```

## Skills Section Format

Balance leadership and technical skills:
```markdown
## Skills

**Leadership**: Team building, hiring & interviewing, performance management, mentoring, conflict resolution, succession planning
**Management**: Agile/Scrum, roadmap planning, resource allocation, vendor management, budget management
**Technical**: System design, architecture review, technical strategy (maintain credibility without deep implementation details)
**Tools**: Jira, Linear, Lattice, Lever, GitHub, Slack
```

## Experience Bullet Point Guidelines

### Prioritize
- Team growth and scaling (hired X engineers, grew team from Y to Z)
- People development (promotions, career growth, mentoring)
- Retention and engagement metrics
- Delivery achievements (shipped X, reduced Y, improved Z)
- Process improvements (agile adoption, incident response, etc.)
- Cross-functional collaboration (product, design, stakeholders)
- Organizational impact (culture, practices adopted by other teams)
- Business outcomes tied to team's work

### De-emphasize
- Deep technical implementation details
- Individual coding contributions (unless demonstrating technical credibility)
- Administrative tasks

### Example Bullet Points (People & Team)
- Built and scaled engineering team from 8 to 35 engineers across 4 squads, maintaining 90%+ retention rate over 3 years
- Hired 25+ engineers through improved recruiting process, reducing time-to-hire from 60 to 30 days while improving offer acceptance rate to 85%
- Promoted 12 engineers to senior levels and 3 to staff level through structured career development programs and mentorship
- Achieved team engagement score of 4.5/5.0 (company avg 3.8) through regular 1-on-1s, team building, and transparent communication
- Implemented engineering levels and career ladder, providing clear growth paths and reducing ambiguity in promotions

### Example Bullet Points (Delivery & Business)
- Led team delivering 3 major product launches generating $20M ARR, coordinating across product, design, and marketing
- Reduced production incidents by 60% through implementation of on-call rotations, blameless postmortems, and SLO tracking
- Improved sprint velocity by 40% through agile process refinements and reduction of meeting overhead
- Partnered with Product to define technical roadmap, balancing 70% feature work with 30% technical debt reduction

### Example Bullet Points (Technical Credibility)
- Guided architectural decisions for platform migration, enabling team to scale from 100K to 10M users
- Established technical standards and code review practices adopted across 5 engineering teams

## Job Description Tailoring

When job description is provided:
- Match team size and scope to job requirements
- Emphasize relevant domain experience (B2B, B2C, specific industries)
- Highlight management style alignment (servant leadership, etc.)
- Feature relevant delivery methodologies (Agile, Scrum, Kanban)
- Match organizational level (IC manager vs manager of managers)
- Emphasize growth if scaling role, stability if mature org

## What to Balance
- Lead with people and business impact, not technical details
- Show technical credibility without overshadowing leadership
- Demonstrate both "building teams" and "delivering results"
- Balance strategic thinking with execution capability

## What to Minimize
- Deep technical implementation details
- Individual contributor achievements (unless showing progression)
- Technologies and tools (keep at high level)
- Coding projects or side projects

## Career Progression Story

Show clear IC-to-management progression:
- Earlier roles: Technical achievements, first leadership opportunities
- Middle roles: Team lead, people management beginnings
- Recent roles: Full management scope, organizational impact
- Demonstrate intentional move to management track

## Management Metrics to Highlight

- Team size and growth (grew team from X to Y)
- Retention rates
- Engagement/satisfaction scores
- Hiring metrics (time-to-hire, acceptance rate)
- Promotion rates
- Delivery metrics (on-time delivery, velocity improvement)
- Business impact ($, users, efficiency gains)
